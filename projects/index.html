Improving Traceability in Avionic Software

I received the NSERC Experience Award, valued at $5625, to undertake a industrial research project at Safran.

As a member of the Software team, I was responsible for creating software architecture diagrams and updating Airborne Software documentation, adhering to the DO-178B standards. In addition, I worked on improving the architectural and documentation process by devising new methods to create more accurate and consistent documentation. During this process, I developed a Python script to parse avionic requirements and cross-check data flow in the code. Consequently, the program reduced errors in documentation by about 50%.

You can also check out my Research Poster (shown below) for the Institute for Multidisciplinary Design & Innovation at the University of Toronto.
